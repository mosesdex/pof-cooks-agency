Findings

Executive summary: five findings that shape the business

The employ-and-dispatch cook agency is a proven model, the South-South is genuinely underserved, and the instincts behind POF's plan are validated by both industry practice and peer-reviewed research.

The market gap is real

Every serious competitor (iKooK, Chef4Me, Kimolar, Workmate) concentrates on Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt. None has meaningful supply in Benin City, Warri, Asaba, or Yenagoa. iKooK lists exactly one chef in Edo State. Verified

Pricing benchmarks exist

Per-day chef hire in Nigeria runs ₦25,000–₦200,000 by seniority; freelance event cooks earn ₦30,000–₦100,000 per event; household cooks earn ₦60,000–₦220,000 monthly. A defensible South-South rate card can be built directly from these numbers. Verified

Scope-lock protects cooks

Locking guest count and food quantity before the job, treating it as a minimum guarantee, and billing overages in full is established catering-industry practice — exactly the abuse-prevention system POF envisioned. Verified

Value bundling beats policing

Peer-reviewed marketplace economics confirms POF's anti-poaching instinct: contracts are only a backstop; the winning strategy is bundling ingredient sourcing, replacement guarantees, escrow, and insurance so a poached cook alone delivers less than the agency does. Verified

There is a legal gate first

Employing workers and supplying them to third parties makes POF a licensed labour contractor under the Nigerian Labour Act. A Recruiter's Licence (₦200,000, 2-year validity), NSITF, pension, and written cook contracts are required before revenue. Verified

The bottom line

Launch legally (licence first), price on the two-axis grid (cook tier × household size × frequency), lock scope in the app before dispatch, and make the POF bundle irreplaceable. Budget ₦2M–₦5.5M compliance setup and target 25–40% gross margin.

Four market takeaways

  • Employ-and-dispatch is rare in Nigeria. Incumbents are either marketplaces (iKooK, Chef4Me) or match-and-place recruiters (Workmate) where the client becomes the employer. POF keeping cooks on its own payroll follows the international staffing-industry template. Verified
  • Pricing opacity is the norm. Domestic-staffing incumbents hide prices behind contact forms; transparent in-app pricing is a genuine differentiator. Reported
  • The market is bigger than the elite. Chef4Me explicitly targets middle-class households with tiered pricing. Reported
  • City wage dynamics favor POF's cost base. Lagos pays 20–40% above other cities; South-South premium demand is oil-driven (oil-camp catering in Port Harcourt/Warri pays ₦250k–₦500k/month). General-market cook wages in POF's cities sit below Lagos benchmarks. Reported

What this means for POF

POF enters as the first employ-and-dispatch cook agency purpose-built for the South-South, with three structural advantages over incumbents:

  • Lower labor cost than Lagos players, with premium pockets (oil-adjacent Warri demand) available for upselling.
  • Transparent app pricing against contact-form incumbents.
  • Existing POF food-delivery brand and user base in-region, so cook-hire launches into a warm audience rather than cold.

Caution: the refuted Dominion claim cuts both ways: less incumbent competition than searches implied, but also less independently validated demand. Run cheap demand tests before scaling the pool (see Risks).

Structure

The business model — agency as employer of record

The structural template comes from the global staffing industry: the agency, not the client, is the legal employer of the assigned workers. Verified · American Staffing Association

POF (the agency) owns

  • Employment contracts, wages, and payroll taxes
  • Recruitment, vetting, and training of the cook pool
  • Conduct standards, discipline, and replacement
  • Statutory obligations — NSITF, pension, PAYE
  • Dispatch, scheduling, and quality control

The customer owns

  • Day-to-day supervision of the work being done
  • A safe worksite (kitchen, utilities, access)
  • Accurate scope declaration — guest count, food quantity, hours
  • Payment through the app, including approved overages

Why this beats a marketplace

  • POF controls quality — cooks are trained employees, not loose freelancers
  • Customers get a replacement guarantee, not a no-show risk
  • Cooks get stable income, statutory protections, and career tiers
  • The employment relationship is itself an anti-poaching moat (Section 06)

Service lines at launch

Home day-cook (one cook, one day, family meals) · Event cooking (teams of 2–5 cooks for weddings, parties, corporate functions) · Business/canteen staffing (monthly, per-cook) · Monthly household retainers (2×/week up to full-time). Customers choose per-day or monthly commercial terms — the two billing modes POF planned from the start.

The costing formula

Booking price = (day_rate[tier] × num_cooks × num_days)
              + transport_surcharge(zone)
              + overtime(hours_over × 1.5 | sunday_holiday × 2.0)
              + statutory_load (NSITF 1% · pension · PAYE)
              + overhead_and_margin (target 25–40% gross)

Cost inputs you must not forget

  • Transport: allowances run ₦15,000–₦60,000/month per worker. Price it as a separate zone-based line item: Kimolar applies location surcharges; Gary's Catering bills distance-based fuel fees. Reported
  • Benefits load: meals, insurance, and service charges add 30–80% above base salary at full load. Reported
  • Overtime norms: weekdays 1.5×, Sundays and public holidays 2×; bake the multipliers into event pricing. Reported
  • Floor references: casual daily labor ≈ ₦4,500/day (2026 guard benchmark); PayScale implies ≈ ₦16,000–17,000 per 8-hour day for a bare kitchen chef. A skilled dispatched cook prices well above both. Reported

Take-rate benchmark

Urban Company, the world's reference hyperlocal services platform, charges a 20–25% commission per booking. Reported

Because POF is the employer (not a commission marketplace), the equivalent is the gross margin between the customer price and the fully-loaded cook cost. Target 25–40% after cook pay, transport, and statutory load.

Safeguards

Protecting your cooks — scope agreements & overage charges

POF's plan (fix headcount and food quantity before the job, charge for breaches) is standard practice in catering and staffing. Seven concrete mechanisms, each with precedent.

  • Minimum-guarantee headcount. Gary's Catering requires the final guest count 10 days before an event; it becomes a minimum guarantee that cannot be lowered afterward. Verified POF version: guest count, menu, and quantity lock at booking confirmation; changes only via in-app re-quote.
  • Overage counting and billing. The caterer counts actual attendance and bills in full for guests above the guarantee. Verified POF version: the dispatched cook logs actuals in the app; overage auto-bills at a premium rate (1.25–1.5× per head); the premium is the deterrent.
  • Deposit + balance structure. Industry template: 25% deposit at booking, balance due 5 days pre-event; cancellation within 7 days owes the full balance; deposits non-refundable after 30 days. Reported This protects committed labor.
  • Job-duty lock. Staffing agreements bar clients from changing a dispatched worker's duties without agency consent, and require a safe worksite; ASA model agreements allocate site-safety training, protective equipment, and injury recordkeeping between agency and client. Verified POF version: the booking lists exactly what the cook will do: "also wash the house" or "cook for 20 more people" requires a paid in-app change order.
  • Overtime multipliers. Weekday overtime 1.5×, Sundays/holidays 2×, applied automatically by the pricing engine when a job runs past booked hours. Reported
  • Liability allocation. Staffing agreements use indemnification clauses assigning each risk to the party that controls it, backed by workers' compensation. Reported Pair with NSITF coverage (Section 07) for injuries.
  • In-app incident reporting. Give cooks a panic/incident button and a post-job condition report; repeated customer violations trigger account suspension. (POF implementation recommendation building on the verified mechanisms above.)
Retention Strategy

Anti-disintermediation — stopping customers from poaching cooks

The sharpest strategic question, answered in three layers. The headline from peer-reviewed research (Hagiu & Wright, Management Science): policing alone fails; value bundling wins, contracts are the backstop, product mechanics close the gaps.

Expect poaching as a structural feature, not an anomaly

Leakage risk is highest exactly in POF's category: repeat transactions between the same counterparties, in-person delivery, scope negotiated face-to-face. Verified Homejoy collapsed partly because its best cleaners left once they had amassed regular customers — anti-leakage design must be built in from launch, because early low leakage breeds complacency. Reported

Layer 1 — The main defense

Make the bundle worth more than the cook alone

The first and main anti-leakage priority is investing in transaction benefits beyond discovery: escrow, insurance, payment facilities, guarantees. Verified Platforms retain users by bundling trust features people forfeit when going direct (Turo's $750k insurance is the canonical example). Verified

What POF bundles so a poached cook delivers less alone — the mechanism behind the outcome POF predicted ("what the cook delivers will not be the same"):

  • Ingredient sourcing & quality control: POF supplies or verifies ingredients at agency rates; a direct-hired cook loses the supply chain.
  • Replacement guarantee: cook sick? POF dispatches a substitute same-day. A direct hire has no backup.
  • Team scaling: events need 2–5 coordinated cooks; only the agency can assemble the team.
  • Escrow & dispute resolution: the customer's money is protected; direct cash deals aren't.
  • Insurance / NSITF coverage: injury or property damage is carried by the agency.
  • Standardized recipes, menus, and QC inspections: the agency playbook, not the individual's.
  • Ratings history: cooks forfeit earned reputation (and the bookings it drives) by leaving; customers forfeit vetted-cook access. Reported
Layer 2 — The backstop

Contracts: non-solicitation and buyout fees

  • Client-side: every client signs a services agreement with a non-solicitation clause barring direct hire of POF cooks Reported · standard staffing practice, plus a conversion/buyout fee for permanent hires. Industry norms: ≈10% of the worker's annual salary, or sliding-scale fees that shrink with tenure and are often waived after 520–1,040 assignment hours (≈3–6 months full-time). Verified The ASA defends these fees as recovering recruitment and onboarding investment. Verified
  • POF version: "Hire your cook permanently for a one-time fee of 1–1.5× their monthly salary; waived after 12 months of bookings."
  • Cook-side: the employment agreement includes a non-solicitation clause barring the cook from becoming a client's direct hire without POF approval. Reported

Nigerian enforceability limits: critical drafting constraints

  • Restraint-of-trade covenants are presumptively unenforceable in Nigeria; courts enforce them only if reasonable in business scope, geography, and duration. Reported · ICLG / Jackson Etti & Edu
  • The FCCPA caps enforceable restraints at 2 years maximum. Reported
  • Courts favor covenants where the worker received consideration — pair clauses with a retention bonus or benefits. Reported
  • You cannot stop a former cook from using cooking skills learned on the job; you need a protectable interest (client lists, recipes, confidential pricing) and narrow scope. Reported
  • The shape that survives: 12–24 months, POF's four cities only, limited to clients the cook actually served through POF. A blanket "never cook for anyone" clause dies in court.
Layer 3 — The product

Mechanics that keep transactions in the app

  • All payments through the app: no cash to cooks (Chef4Me operates this way). Reported
  • Escrow: hold the customer's payment; release to the cook's wallet only after completion confirmation. Verified · recommended mechanism
  • Contact masking: in-app chat with masked phone numbers; flag trigger words ("call me", "WhatsApp", digit strings) for review. Reported Use it lightly; heavy-handed policing (commission nags, ban threats, contact blocking) has limited effectiveness and can backfire (the Airbnb/BlaBlaCar experience). Verified
  • Accept some leakage. The profit-maximizing platform always tolerates partial leakage; eliminating it entirely costs more than it saves. Price the take rate expecting some direct deals. Verified
  • Loyalty economics: discount the 10th booking, pay cook tenure bonuses, and let both sides accumulate visible ratings capital they would lose by leaving. Reported
Compliance

Nigerian legal & regulatory checklist

Complete these before taking bookings. Employing cooks and supplying them to third parties is a licensed activity in Nigeria.

Recruiter's Licence (labour contractor category) Verified · nelex.gov.ng

Any company that employs workers to make them available to a third party needs a Recruiter's Licence from the Federal Ministry of Labour and Employment under the Labour Act CAP L1 2004 (ss. 23, 24, 25, 71, 88(e,f,g)). Fresh domestic licence: ₦5,000 application + ₦200,000 fee, valid 2 years; renewal ₦100,000/1yr or ₦200,000/3yrs. Apply via the NELEX portal (~2 weeks with complete documents). Requirements include incorporation certificate, insurance bond (₦1.5M–₦5M by sector/headcount), PFA registration, NSITF registration, health insurance evidence, employee contracts, an employee handbook, training policies, and ITF registration. Violations range from reprimand to licence withdrawal and prosecution.

NSITF · Employee Compensation Act Reported

Every employer with ≥1 employee must register and contribute 1% of total monthly payroll. Coverage extends to casual and contract workers; per-day dispatched cooks are covered. Non-compliance blocks the compliance certificate needed for government and corporate contracts, which matters when POF pursues corporate canteen clients.

Pension · PRA 2014 Reported

Obligations attach to casual workers once employed 3+ months, or when repeated short contracts add up to continuous service. A standing cook pool crosses this threshold quickly; budget pension contributions from the start.

PAYE remittance Reported

The agency, as employer, deducts and remits income tax for cooks once annualized income crosses the personal relief threshold; this duty sits with POF, never the client.

Written employment contracts Reported

Labour Act s.7 requires a written contract within 3 months of starting work. Cooks performing manual labour are "workers" entitled to the Act's statutory minimum protections.

Watch item: NSITF successor bill Reported

The NSITF Act is slated for replacement by a Nigeria Social Security Trust Fund Bill (expected 2026) establishing a comprehensive compensation regime for work-related death, injury, disease, or disability. Track it.

Setup capital estimate

Budget roughly ₦2M–₦5.5M for licence + insurance bond + registrations before payroll begins. This is front-loaded, unavoidable, and the licence is also a marketing asset: "a licensed labour contractor" is a trust signal none of the informal competition can claim.

Guests/staff to feed, menu, food quantity, venue, kitchen facilities, hours, number of cooks. For large events: an optional paid pre-visit (Kimolar charges ₦5k virtual to ₦30k in-home, 50% credited toward service). Reported

Minimum-guarantee scope, overage rates, cancellation tiers, non-solicitation + buyout clause — one tap, legally binding, stored per booking.

Pay deposit into escrow

25–50% deposit at booking; balance auto-charged before dispatch (5 days ahead for events). Reported basis

Customer confirms → escrow releases to the cook's wallet → both sides rate each other on dimensions (food quality, punctuality, professionalism / customer conduct).

  • Earnings dashboard + one-tap acceptance: platforms with high earnings visibility and low acceptance friction retain 40–60% more supply in the first 90 days. Reported
  • Job checklists with photo documentation; incident/panic reporting.

Platform mechanics

  • All payments in-app; wallet payouts; no cash.
  • Contact masking in chat with trigger-word flagging — applied gently (Layer 3).
  • Ratings as lock-in capital for both sides.

Caveats

Risks & caveats

What the evidence does not support, where the data is thin, and what can sink the launch.

Refuted during verification

The claim that Dominion Consulting operates in Warri and Benin City failed adversarial verification 0-3. Refuted Do not assume incumbent presence there; equally, do not assume proven demand. Run cheap demand tests (a waitlist inside POF, event-caterer partnerships) before hiring the full pool.

Wage data is thin for these cities

Every salary source is Lagos-skewed or small-sample (Glassdoor: 27 profiles; PayScale: 6, stale since 2022; Mywage 2021 pre-devaluation figures refuted). Run a 2-week local survey — ask 20–30 cooks in Benin City and Warri their day and monthly rates — before finalizing the rate card.

Naira volatility

All ₦ figures are 2025–26 and will drift. Index the rate card quarterly and build repricing into retainer contracts.

Compliance is front-loaded

The licence + bond (≈₦2M–₦5.5M) is real money before revenue. Skipping it invites prosecution and licence-based shutdown once the business is visible, and visibility is the point of an app.

Leakage cannot be zeroed

The verified economics finding: optimal platforms tolerate partial leakage. Verified Measure it (repeat-customer booking decay per cook) and manage it; don't chase it to zero.

Employment cost discipline

As employer of record, POF carries payroll through demand troughs. Start with 4–6 cooks, use a bench of vetted on-call cooks for event spikes, and only convert to full-time as retainer revenue locks in.
